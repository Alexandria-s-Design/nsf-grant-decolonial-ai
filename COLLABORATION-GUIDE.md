# Collaboration Guide

## üë• Team Roles & Responsibilities

### Lead Author
- **Responsibilities**: Project vision, final manuscript decisions, correspondence with journal
- **Commitment**: 10-15 hours/week during active writing phases
- **Deliverables**: Introduction, Discussion, final integration

### Co-Authors
- **Responsibilities**: Section writing, literature review, data analysis, revision feedback
- **Commitment**: 5-10 hours/week during assigned phases
- **Deliverables**: Assigned sections, citation verification, peer review

### Research Assistants
- **Responsibilities**: Literature search, data extraction, bibliography management
- **Commitment**: 5-8 hours/week
- **Deliverables**: Annotated bibliographies, data tables, preliminary drafts

### AI Assistants
- **Responsibilities**: Draft generation, synthesis, citation formatting, quality checks
- **Workflow**: Generate ‚Üí Human Review ‚Üí Revise ‚Üí Validate ‚Üí Commit
- **Limitations**: No autonomous decisions, always supervised by human authors

## üìù Writing Process

### Drafting Protocol
1. **First Draft**: Author writes 70-80% complete section
2. **AI Enhancement**: AI polishes language, adds citations, formats
3. **Peer Review**: Co-author reviews for accuracy and coherence
4. **Revision**: Author incorporates feedback
5. **Final Review**: Lead author approves for integration

### Citation Management
- **Tool**: Zotero (shared library)
- **Style**: APA 7th Edition
- **Protocol**: Every claim must have citation, verify DOIs, maintain shared bibliography

### Version Control
- **Branch Strategy**: 
  - `main` = production-ready manuscript
  - `develop` = integration branch
  - `feature/section-name` = individual section work
- **Commit Messages**: Clear, descriptive (e.g., "Add literature review section 2.1")
- **Pull Requests**: Required for all merges, minimum 1 reviewer

## üö® Conflict Resolution

### Disagreements on Content
1. Document both perspectives
2. Consult relevant literature
3. Discuss in team meeting
4. Lead author makes final decision
5. Document rationale in meeting notes

### Timeline Concerns
1. Communicate delays immediately
2. Propose adjusted timeline
3. Reallocate tasks if needed
4. Update project documentation

## üéØ Authorship Criteria

To qualify as an author, contributors must meet **all four** ICMJE criteria:

1. Substantial contributions to conception/design OR acquisition/analysis/interpretation of data
2. Drafting the work OR revising it critically for important intellectual content
3. Final approval of the version to be published
4. Agreement to be accountable for all aspects of the work

**Acknowledgments**: Contributors not meeting all criteria will be acknowledged appropriately.
