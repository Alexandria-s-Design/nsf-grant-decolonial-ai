## Proposal Overview

This NSF grant proposal seeks $750,000 over 3 years to develop, validate, and disseminate **The Right Path**, a comprehensive decolonial framework for AI integration in K-12 education.

### Target NSF Programs

1. **NSF EHR Core Research (ECR)**: Advances fundamental research on STEM teaching and learning
2. **Advancing Informal STEM Learning (AISL)**: Develops community-based AI learning experiences
3. **Computer Science for All (CSforAll)**: Expands equitable CS education with culturally sustaining approaches
4. **NSF INCLUDES**: Addresses systemic barriers to broadening participation in STEM

## Key Features of This Proposal

### 1. Theoretical Innovation
- **First comprehensive decolonial framework** for K-12 AI education
- Synthesizes Indigenous pedagogies, decolonial theory, and critical AI studies
- Operationalizes abstract principles into concrete educational practices

### 2. Methodological Rigor
- **Participatory Action Research** with Community Research Teams
- **Mixed-methods design** (qual + quan) with 30 partner schools
- **Novel assessment**: AI & Human Flourishing Readiness Index
- **Indigenous research methodologies** for tribal partners

### 3. Broader Impacts Excellence
- **10,000+ students** across diverse contexts (65% students of color)
- **500+ educators** receiving professional development
- **Community-centered**: 70% of budget flows to partners
- **Open Educational Resources**: Free curriculum, toolkit, professional learning

### 4. Equity & Justice Focus
- Centers communities historically excluded from technology design
- **Indigenous data sovereignty** protocols
- **Community governance** of research process
- Asset-based (not deficit) approaches

### 5. Scalability & Sustainability
- Replicable framework adoptable nationwide
- Open-source curriculum and resources
- Train-the-trainer model (50 teacher-leaders)
- Policy influence (federal, state, local)

## Research Questions

**RQ1: Framework Development & Validation**
- How can decolonial epistemologies inform AI integration in K-12 education?
- What pedagogical practices, curricular approaches, and institutional policies constitute a decolonial AI framework?

**RQ2: Implementation & Outcomes**
- What are the effects of decolonial AI pedagogy on student learning, engagement, and CS identity?
- What institutional conditions enable or constrain decolonial AI implementation?

**RQ3: Equity & Impact**
- How does decolonial AI pedagogy broaden participation in computing?
- What are differential impacts across diverse cultural contexts?

## Proposal Strengths

### Competitive Advantages
1. **Timely & Urgent**: Addresses national need for ethical AI in education (White House AI Bill of Rights, ED AI Guidance)
2. **Community-Centered**: Authentic partnerships with marginalized communities (not extractive research)
3. **Theoretically Grounded**: Deep engagement with decolonial theory, Indigenous pedagogies, critical AI studies
4. **Methodologically Rigorous**: Mixed-methods, participatory, longitudinal design
5. **Practically Useful**: Delivers tools educators need now (curriculum, PD, vendor audit)
6. **Scalable**: Replicable model for nationwide adoption
7. **Sustainable**: Infrastructure outlives grant period
8. **Policy-Relevant**: Informs federal/state AI education policies

### Potential Reviewer Concerns & Responses

**Concern**: "Decolonial theory is too abstract for K-12 practice"
- **Response**: Framework operationalizes principles into concrete pedagogical practices, curriculum, and policies. Pilot work demonstrates feasibility.

**Concern**: "Participatory research is methodologically weak"
- **Response**: Robust mixed-methods design with quantitative rigor (HLM, quasi-experimental) + qualitative depth. Participatory approach enhances—not diminishes—validity.

**Concern**: "Difficult to implement across diverse contexts"
- **Response**: Maximum variation sampling (urban, rural, tribal) explicitly examines contextual factors. Framework designed for adaptability, not one-size-fits-all.

**Concern**: "Ambitious scope for 3-year timeline"
- **Response**: Detailed timeline with realistic milestones. Strong project management (full-time Project Director). Pilot work demonstrates capacity.

**Concern**: "Community data sovereignty may limit data sharing"
- **Response**: Tiered access model balances open science with community rights. Majority of data (Tiers 1-2) will be publicly shared; only culturally sensitive data (Tier 3) restricted.
